Madhuramveppu

Bride's Side · Traditional Pre-Wedding Ceremony

Madhuramveppu is a cherished pre-wedding tradition observed among Kerala Syrian Orthodox Christian families. The term "Madhuramveppu" literally means "serving sweetness," symbolising the sharing of joy, love, and blessings before the wedding ceremony.

Event · 03 — The Big Day

Wedding Ceremony & Reception

St. Mary's Jacobite Syrian Orthodox Church
Mulavoor, Kerala

The sacred wedding ceremony, celebrated according to the holy traditions of the Kerala Syrian Orthodox Church, will begin with the Modhiravazhvu Susrusha (Blessing and Exchange of Rings), symbolising the official betrothal and the couple's commitment before God and the Church. This is followed by the solemn Second Susrusha — the Holy Crowning Ceremony — where the bride and groom receive blessings through prayers, the tying of the Minnu (sacred wedding pendant), the placing of the Manthrakodi, and the priestly blessings that sanctify their union as a sacred Christian marriage.

The ceremony will be followed by a joyous reception at the Church Auditorium.
